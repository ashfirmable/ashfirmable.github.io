Week in Review  ·  Week of 4 May 2026

The week saw a sharp MQL bounce-back, hitting 99 leads (+48% week-on-week, +40% versus the 4-week rolling average of 70). Paid Search led with 41 MQLs, followed by Direct (27) and Organic Search (25).

However, the surge did not flow through to new opportunities. Only 23 opps were created (−36% week-on-week, −27% vs avg), and pipeline value created came in at $24.5k — well below the 4-week average of $35k. This points to either lead-quality drift or a sales follow-up lag worth monitoring.

Closed Won held steady. 18 deals worth $30k closed, just above the 4-week average of $28.7k. Direct remains the largest source of closed-won value across the period.

Paid spend was essentially flat at $4,273 (−1% week-on-week), while traffic rose to 1,579 users (+13%), so CPL improved meaningfully to $43 — versus the prior week's $64 and the 4-week average of $58.

To watch: whether the elevated MQL volume converts into opportunities over the next two weeks. If conversion stays soft, dig into Paid Search lead intent and SDR follow-up cadence.
